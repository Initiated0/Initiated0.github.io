Sign Language App

The idea is based on the fact that people with hearing disabilities have their own sign language, but normal people who don't know the signs find it difficult to communicate with the former group. To solve this communication problem, we devised a solution that will transform the signs into audio.

The project had three components; Firstly, we needed to recognize the signs. Secondly, convert the recognized signs into texts. Lastly, turn those texts into audio speech.

We used machine learning models to recognize the signs by using VGG16 with pre-trained weights and biases, trained on IMAGENET dataset. Since it was a classification problem, we used one of the classics. But the model overfitted, took a lot of time to process and in the testing phase, it had a high percentage of inaccuracies. We switched to MobileNet architecture that had 4.2 million parameters (in contrast, VGG16 had 17 million parameters ). This lightweight model was better in training as well as in testing, surpassing other models that we also tried later(ResNet, Inception, Xception etc).

There were 29 classes in American Sign Language(ASL) and the model's highest probability of prediction enabled us to choose a class. It generated the letter we needed. Space between letters indicated the completion of a word.

Afterward, we had to convert the text into speech. For this task, we used google's text-to-speech API. We integrated the API, and combined all the components inside a Web App, which was designed using Flask framework.
